## 💼 Remote Work Culture & Etiquette

We're a **remote-first company**, which means we've built our culture around distributed teamwork. Here's how we make it work:

### Communication Principles

#### 1. **Asynchronous First, Synchronous When Needed**

We respect that you're in different time zones. Here's our approach:

**Asynchronous Communication (Preferred):**
- Use Slack for messages that don't need immediate responses
- Document decisions and information in shared spaces
- Give people time to respond—no pressure for instant replies
- Perfect for: Updates, questions, sharing information

**Synchronous Communication (When Necessary):**
- Schedule meetings at times that work for everyone (or rotate times)
- Use video calls for complex discussions or relationship building
- Meetings should have clear agendas and outcomes
- Perfect for: Brainstorming, problem-solving, team bonding

#### 2. **Over-Communication is Better Than Under-Communication**

In a remote environment, it's easy for miscommunication to happen. We combat this by:
- Sharing more context than you think necessary
- Asking clarifying questions when something isn't clear
- Documenting decisions and action items
- Following up on conversations with written summaries

#### 3. **Respect Boundaries & Time Zones**

We have team members across the globe. Here's how we respect each other's time:
- Don't expect immediate responses outside business hours
- Schedule meetings that work for all participants (or record them)
- Respect "do not disturb" hours and vacation time
- Use "away" status in Slack when you're offline
- Understand that someone's "morning" might be your "evening"

### Meeting Etiquette

#### Before the Meeting
- **Send an agenda** at least 24 hours in advance
- **Include time zone information** (e.g., "2 PM EST / 7 PM GMT")
- **Make it optional** if attendance isn't critical
- **Record the meeting** so people in different time zones can catch up

#### During the Meeting
- **Cameras ON** 📹 - This is non-negotiable! Cameras help us maintain personal connection and show respect for colleagues
- **Be on time** - Respect everyone's schedule
- **Minimize distractions** - Find a quiet space, silence notifications
- **Participate actively** - Speak up, ask questions, share ideas
- **Listen actively** - Don't multitask, give speakers your full attention
- **Use video, not just audio** - Face-to-face connection matters

#### After the Meeting
- **Share notes** with action items and decisions
- **Follow up** on action items promptly
- **Record and share** the meeting for those who couldn't attend
- **Thank people** for their time and contributions

### Slack Etiquette

**DO:**
- ✅ Use threads to keep conversations organized
- ✅ Use reactions (👍 😂 ❤️) to acknowledge messages
- ✅ Share relevant updates and wins
- ✅ Ask questions—we encourage learning
- ✅ Use appropriate channels for different topics
- ✅ Respect "do not disturb" status

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Expect immediate responses (we're not always online!)
- ❌ Send the same message across multiple channels
- ❌ Use @channel or @here unless it's urgent
- ❌ Have sensitive conversations in public channels
- ❌ Assume tone from text—ask for clarification if confused
- ❌ Message people outside their work hours

### Email Etiquette

**Use email for:**
- Formal communications and documentation
- Detailed information that needs to be archived
- Communications with external parties
- When Slack isn't appropriate

**Email best practices:**
- Keep subject lines clear and specific
- Use "To:" for people who need to act; "CC:" for people who need to know
- Respond within 24 business hours
- Use clear formatting for readability
- Avoid "reply all" unless necessary

## 🤝 Building Relationships Across Distances

### Connecting with Your Team

**Regular 1-on-1s:**
- Schedule weekly or bi-weekly meetings with your manager
- Use this time to discuss goals, challenges, and growth
- It's okay to share personal updates—we're humans first!

**Team Bonding:**
- Participate in virtual team events and celebrations
- Share wins and milestones with the team
- Celebrate birthdays and work anniversaries
- Join optional social channels and activities

**Getting to Know Colleagues:**
- Attend virtual coffee chats and informal hangouts
- Share a bit about yourself in introductions
- Ask colleagues about their backgrounds and interests
- Participate in team traditions and rituals

### Celebrating Differences

**Cultural Awareness:**
- Learn about your colleagues' cultures and backgrounds
- Ask respectful questions about traditions and celebrations
- Participate in cultural celebrations when invited
- Be mindful of different holidays and observances

**Language Inclusivity:**
- Speak clearly and use simple language in meetings
- Don't assume everyone's first language is English
- Be patient with accents and language differences
- Offer to repeat or clarify if someone doesn't understand

**Accessibility & Inclusion:**
- Ask about accessibility needs and accommodations
- Use captions and transcripts for videos
- Be mindful of different communication styles
- Adapt your approach to work for everyone

## 💡 Best Practices for Remote Success

### Setting Up Your Workspace

**Create a Professional Environment:**
- Find a quiet space for meetings and calls
- Ensure good lighting (natural light is best!)
- Use a clean, neutral background for video calls
- Minimize distractions and background noise

**Tech Setup:**
- Invest in a good microphone and camera
- Test your audio and video before important meetings
- Have a backup internet connection if possible
- Keep your equipment updated and maintained

### Work-Life Balance

**Establish Boundaries:**
- Set clear work hours and stick to them
- Create a "commute" ritual to start and end your day
- Take breaks throughout the day
- Don't work outside your scheduled hours unless necessary

**Stay Connected:**
- Participate in team events and social activities
- Build relationships with colleagues
- Take advantage of company resources and support
- Reach out when you need help or support

### Productivity Tips

**Stay Organized:**
- Use project management tools effectively
- Keep your workspace organized and clutter-free
- Maintain a to-do list and prioritize tasks
- Document your work for transparency

**Communication:**
- Over-communicate rather than under-communicate
- Update your status regularly
- Share progress and blockers with your team
- Ask for help when you need it
