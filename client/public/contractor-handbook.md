## Getting Started

### Your First Week

**Day 1-2: Onboarding**
- Complete all required paperwork and agreements
- Receive access to communication platforms and tools
- Schedule introductory call with your project manager
- Review project scope and deliverables
- Set up your remote workspace

**Day 3-5: Orientation**
- Attend team introduction meetings
- Complete required training modules
- Familiarize yourself with company processes and workflows
- Ask questions and clarify expectations
- Submit your first status update

### Key Contacts

- **Project Manager**: Your primary point of contact for project-related questions
- **HR Coordinator**: For administrative, payment, and policy questions
- **Technical Support**: For IT and tool-related issues
- **Compliance Officer**: For legal and confidentiality concerns

### Contractor Agreement Highlights

As a contractor, you are:
- **Not eligible for employee benefits** (health insurance, retirement plans, paid time off)
- **Required to maintain confidentiality** of all company information
- **Expected to deliver quality work** according to agreed-upon specifications
- **Responsible for your own equipment and workspace**

## Work Environment & Setup

### Home Office Essentials

**Physical Setup**
- Dedicated workspace with a desk and ergonomic chair
- Reliable internet connection (minimum 25 Mbps download, 5 Mbps upload)
- Quiet area free from distractions and background noise
- Proper lighting to reduce eye strain
- Backup power source or UPS for internet router

**Equipment Requirements**
- Computer (laptop or desktop) meeting minimum specifications
- Webcam and microphone (built-in or external)
- Headphones for calls and focus work
- Phone for emergency contact
- Backup internet option (mobile hotspot or secondary connection)

### Ergonomics & Health

**Proper Desk Setup**
- Monitor at eye level, 20-26 inches away
- Keyboard and mouse at elbow height
- Feet flat on floor or footrest
- Chair supporting natural spine curve
- Desk height allowing 90-degree arm angle

**Taking Care of Your Body**
- Take a 5-10 minute break every hour
- Practice the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds
- Stretch regularly throughout the day
- Stay hydrated and maintain good posture
- Consider a standing desk or desk converter

## Communication Standards

### Response Times

| Communication Type | Expected Response Time |
|---|---|
| Urgent messages (Slack/Teams) | Within 1 hour |
| Regular messages | Within 4 business hours |
| Email | Within 24 business hours |
| Project updates | As scheduled (typically weekly) |

### Communication Channels

**Slack/Teams** - For quick questions, daily updates, and team chat
**Email** - For formal communications, documentation, and records
**Video Calls** - For meetings, complex discussions, and relationship building
**Project Management Tool** - For task tracking and deliverable updates

### Meeting Etiquette

**Before the Meeting**
- Join 2-3 minutes early
- Test your audio and video
- Minimize distractions
- Have relevant materials ready
- Ensure good lighting on your face

**During the Meeting**
- Keep camera on (unless otherwise stated)
- Mute when not speaking
- Avoid multitasking
- Take notes
- Participate actively

**After the Meeting**
- Send action items summary if you're leading
- Follow up on assigned tasks promptly
- Share meeting notes with attendees

### Email Best Practices

- Use clear, professional subject lines
- Keep emails concise and well-organized
- Use bullet points for multiple items
- Proofread before sending
- Use "Reply All" sparingly
- Include relevant context for new recipients
- Respond within 24 business hours

## Time Management & Productivity

### Recommended Work Schedule

**Standard Hours**
- Monday-Friday, 9 AM - 5 PM (your local time)
- Flexibility for time zone differences
- Coordinate with your project manager for non-standard hours

**Flexibility**
- Discuss flexible hours with your project manager
- Maintain consistent availability during core hours
- Notify team of schedule changes in advance
- Ensure project deadlines are met regardless of schedule

### Productivity Tips

**Creating a Routine**
- Start work at the same time each day
- Dress professionally (even at home)
- Eat breakfast before work
- Have a morning ritual to transition into work mode
- End work at a consistent time

**Staying Focused**
- Use the Pomodoro Technique (25 min work, 5 min break)
- Turn off non-essential notifications
- Use website blockers for distracting sites during work
- Keep your desk organized
- Use background music or white noise if helpful

**Managing Energy**
- Schedule important tasks during peak energy hours
- Take proper lunch breaks away from your desk
- Avoid back-to-back meetings
- Build in buffer time between tasks
- Practice time blocking

### Tracking Your Time

- Use time tracking software as required by your contract
- Log time daily (don't wait until end of week)
- Be accurate and honest in time reporting
- Include detailed descriptions of work completed
- Submit timesheets by the agreed deadline

### Technical Support

**Getting Help**
1. Check the help documentation first
2. Search for solutions online
3. Contact technical support with:
   - Detailed description of the issue
   - Steps you've already tried
   - Screenshots or error messages
   - Your device and software information

**Common Issues & Solutions**
- **Connection drops**: Check internet speed, restart router, contact ISP
- **Audio issues**: Test microphone settings, check permissions, update drivers
- **Video problems**: Check camera settings, ensure good lighting, update drivers
- **Software crashes**: Restart application, clear cache, update software

### Data Backup

- Back up important files daily
- Use cloud storage for automatic backup
- Keep local backups on external drive
- Test your backup system monthly
- Maintain 3-2-1 backup strategy (3 copies, 2 different media, 1 offsite)

## Security & Confidentiality

### Information Security

**Protecting Company Data**
- Never share passwords or access credentials
- Use strong, unique passwords (minimum 12 characters)
- Enable two-factor authentication on all accounts
- Lock your computer when away from desk
- Use VPN on public WiFi
- Don't discuss confidential information in public spaces

**Device Security**
- Keep operating system updated
- Install and maintain antivirus software
- Enable firewall protection
- Disable automatic WiFi connections
- Use full-disk encryption
- Set up automatic lock after 5 minutes of inactivity

**Internet Safety**
- Avoid public WiFi for sensitive work
- Use a password-protected home network
- Disable file sharing on your computer
- Don't download files from untrusted sources
- Be cautious with email attachments
- Verify sender identity before opening links

### Confidentiality Agreement

As a contractor, you agree to:
- Keep all company information confidential
- Not disclose client information
- Not share project details on social media
- Return all company materials upon contract end
- Respect intellectual property rights
- Not compete during contract period
- Maintain privacy of other contractors

### Reporting Security Issues

If you suspect a security breach:
1. **Stop** - Don't continue using the affected system
2. **Report** - Contact your project manager and compliance officer immediately
3. **Document** - Write down what happened and when
4. **Cooperate** - Assist with investigation
5. **Follow** - Implement recommended security measures

## Health & Wellness

### Mental Health & Wellbeing

**Preventing Burnout**
- Maintain clear work-life boundaries
- Take regular breaks throughout the day
- Use your full lunch break away from desk
- Don't work on weekends unless necessary
- Take vacation time to recharge
- Maintain social connections

**Isolation Prevention**
- Schedule regular video calls
- Participate in team virtual events
- Join online communities
- Work from coffee shops occasionally
- Maintain friendships outside work
- Consider co-working spaces

### Physical Health

**Daily Movement**
- Stand and stretch every hour
- Take a 15-30 minute walk during lunch
- Do light exercises or yoga
- Use stairs instead of elevator
- Maintain good posture
- Stay hydrated

**Nutrition**
- Eat regular, balanced meals
- Avoid excessive caffeine
- Keep healthy snacks available
- Meal prep on weekends
- Limit energy drinks and sugary foods
- Stay hydrated throughout day

### Work-Life Balance

**Setting Boundaries**
- Define clear work hours
- Create physical separation from work area
- Turn off notifications after hours
- Don't check email on weekends
- Communicate your availability
- Respect others' boundaries

**Time Off**
- Take vacation days to recharge
- Plan time off in advance
- Fully disconnect during time off
- Don't check work communications
- Return refreshed and energized

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: How do I request time off?**
A: Discuss with your project manager at least 2 weeks in advance. Submit formal request through appropriate channels. Ensure coverage for your responsibilities.

**Q: Can I work from different locations?**
A: Yes, with approval from your project manager. Ensure reliable internet and quiet workspace. Notify team of location changes. Maintain security protocols.

**Q: What if I need to take a sick day?**
A: Notify your project manager as soon as possible. Provide updates on your condition and expected return. Arrange for coverage if needed.

**Q: How do I handle time zone differences?**
A: Coordinate with your project manager. Attend core team meetings. Use scheduling tools for meeting times. Be flexible when possible.

### Technical Questions

**Q: What should I do if my internet goes down?**
A: Have a backup plan (mobile hotspot). Notify your team immediately. Update your status as soon as possible. Use offline work if available.

**Q: Can I use my personal computer for work?**
A: Discuss with your project manager. Ensure it meets security requirements. Keep personal and work files separate. Maintain antivirus protection.

**Q: What if I have software compatibility issues?**
A: Contact technical support with details. Try troubleshooting steps provided. Document the issue. Request alternative solutions if needed.

### Payment Questions

**Q: When will I receive my payment?**
A: Payments are processed on [specify day]. Invoices must be submitted by [specify date]. Direct deposit typically takes 1-2 business days.

**Q: What if my payment is late?**
A: Contact accounts payable immediately. Provide invoice number and submission date. Follow up in writing if needed. Escalate if issue persists.

### Policy Questions

**Q: What's the policy on client communication?**
A: Always communicate through official channels. Keep project manager informed. Document all important discussions. Follow confidentiality agreements.

**Q: Can I subcontract work to others?**
A: No, unless explicitly approved in writing. Discuss with project manager if needed. Maintain responsibility for all deliverables. Follow all security protocols.

**Q: What happens if I need to end the contract early?**
A: Provide notice as specified in contract (typically 2 weeks). Complete all outstanding work. Document everything. Return all company materials.

## Contract Termination

### End of Contract Process

**Final 2 Weeks**
- Complete all outstanding work
- Document your processes and findings
- Train replacement if applicable
- Archive all project files
- Prepare final invoice
- Return all company materials

**Final Invoice**
- Include all work completed
- Specify end date
- Include any final expenses
- Submit by deadline
- Keep copy for records

**After Contract Ends**
- Don't access company systems
- Return all equipment and materials
- Maintain confidentiality indefinitely
- Don't solicit company clients
- Provide references if requested
- Stay professional in all communications
