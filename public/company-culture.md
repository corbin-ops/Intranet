# DewClaw Land Company Culture

## Our Culture: Building Excellence Through Diversity & Remote Collaboration

At DewClaw Land, we believe that our greatest strength lies in the diversity of our team. We work with talented professionals across different time zones, backgrounds, and cultures. Our commitment to fostering an inclusive, respectful, and productive remote work environment is central to who we are.

---

## Embracing Diversity

### What Diversity Means to Us

We celebrate differences in:
- **Background & Experience**: Team members from various industries, geographies, and professional backgrounds bring unique perspectives
- **Culture & Ethnicity**: We respect and value cultural differences and traditions
- **Thinking Styles**: We encourage diverse approaches to problem-solving and innovation
- **Work Styles**: We accommodate different work preferences and productivity patterns
- **Life Experiences**: We recognize that everyone brings their whole self to work

### Our Commitment

We are committed to creating a workplace where:
- Every team member feels valued and respected
- Diverse perspectives are actively sought and appreciated
- Inclusion is not just a goal but a daily practice
- Discrimination and bias have no place
- Everyone has equal opportunities for growth and advancement

## Remote Work Culture

### Why We Work Remotely

Remote work at DewClaw Land enables us to:
- Access top talent from around the world
- Provide flexibility for work-life balance
- Reduce environmental impact
- Create a more inclusive workplace for people with different needs
- Foster a culture of trust and accountability

### Core Remote Work Principles

**Trust & Accountability**
- We trust our team members to manage their own time and productivity
- Results matter more than hours logged
- We hold ourselves accountable to our commitments

**Communication & Transparency**
- Clear, timely communication is essential in a remote environment
- We over-communicate rather than under-communicate
- Documentation is important—write things down
- Assume positive intent in all interactions

**Collaboration & Connection**
- Remote doesn't mean isolated—we actively build relationships
- We use synchronous and asynchronous communication effectively
- We create opportunities for team connection and bonding
- We celebrate wins together, even from a distance

## Remote Work Etiquette

### Meeting Expectations

**Camera Policy: Cameras ON for All Meetings**

We believe that seeing each other builds stronger connections and improves communication. Therefore:
- **Cameras should be ON** for all team meetings, one-on-ones, and client calls
- This helps us maintain personal connection despite working remotely
- It demonstrates respect and engagement with the meeting
- If you have technical issues with your camera, notify your manager in advance

**Meeting Preparation**
- Join meetings on time (aim for 2-3 minutes early)
- Test your audio and video before the meeting
- Minimize background distractions
- Use professional backgrounds if needed
- Mute when not speaking to reduce background noise

**Meeting Participation**
- Give the meeting your full attention—no multitasking
- Engage actively in discussions
- Ask questions and contribute ideas
- Respect others' speaking time
- Use hand-raising or chat features to indicate you want to speak

### Communication Guidelines

**Slack & Instant Messaging**
- Use Slack as our primary communication tool
- Respond to messages within a reasonable timeframe (typically within 2-4 hours during business hours)
- Use threads to keep conversations organized
- Respect "Do Not Disturb" status—don't escalate to other channels unless urgent
- Use appropriate channels for different topics

**Email**
- Use email for formal communications and documentation
- Include clear subject lines
- Keep emails professional and concise
- Respond within 24 business hours
- Use email for sensitive or confidential matters

**Asynchronous Communication**
- Not everyone works the same hours—prepare materials in advance
- Record important meetings for those who can't attend live
- Document decisions and action items in writing
- Give people time to respond—don't expect immediate replies

### Time Zone Considerations

**Respecting Different Time Zones**
- We have team members across multiple time zones
- Schedule meetings at times that work for the majority
- Record meetings for those who cannot attend live
- Don't expect immediate responses outside of business hours
- Be flexible and rotate meeting times when possible

**Scheduling Best Practices**
- Use a shared calendar tool and check availability
- Provide meeting agendas in advance
- Send reminders with time zone conversions
- Respect people's personal time—avoid scheduling outside business hours

### Professional Presence

**Workspace & Background**
- Ensure your workspace is professional and distraction-free
- Use a clean, neutral background or a virtual background if needed
- Ensure adequate lighting so your face is clearly visible
- Minimize background noise (pets, children, household sounds)

**Professional Appearance**
- Dress professionally for video meetings
- Maintain good grooming standards
- Avoid wearing pajamas or overly casual clothing during business hours
- First impressions matter—present yourself professionally

**Professionalism in Communication**
- Use professional language in all communications
- Avoid slang, excessive emojis, or casual language in formal settings
- Proofread messages before sending
- Be respectful and courteous in all interactions
- Maintain confidentiality of sensitive information

## Collaboration Across Cultures

### Cultural Awareness & Sensitivity

**Learning About Each Other**
- Take time to learn about your teammates' backgrounds and cultures
- Ask respectful questions about cultural practices and traditions
- Share about your own background and culture
- Celebrate cultural holidays and observances as a team

**Avoiding Stereotypes & Bias**
- Don't make assumptions based on someone's background or appearance
- Recognize that individuals are unique—don't generalize
- Challenge stereotypes when you encounter them
- Speak up if you witness discrimination or bias

**Inclusive Language**
- Use inclusive language that respects all team members
- Avoid idioms or cultural references that may not translate
- Be mindful of different communication styles
- Ask for clarification if you don't understand something

### Building Inclusive Relationships

**Getting to Know Your Team**
- Participate in virtual team events and social activities
- Have one-on-one conversations with teammates
- Show genuine interest in people's lives and perspectives
- Support colleagues during important cultural events

**Creating Psychological Safety**
- Encourage people to be their authentic selves
- Create space for different perspectives and ideas
- Listen without judgment
- Admit mistakes and learn from them together

## Work-Life Balance & Wellness

### Respecting Boundaries

**Personal Time**
- Respect colleagues' personal time and boundaries
- Don't expect responses outside of business hours
- Encourage team members to take breaks and vacation
- Lead by example—maintain your own work-life balance

**Flexibility**
- Remote work offers flexibility—use it wisely
- Communicate your working hours to your team
- Be transparent about when you're available
- Respect different working patterns and schedules

### Mental Health & Wellbeing

**Supporting Each Other**
- Check in on colleagues' wellbeing regularly
- Recognize signs of burnout or stress
- Encourage use of mental health resources
- Create a supportive environment for discussing challenges

**Company Support**
- We provide resources for mental health and wellness
- Flexible schedules to accommodate personal needs
- Encouragement to take time off and recharge
- Open dialogue about work-life balance

---

## Conflict Resolution & Feedback

### Giving & Receiving Feedback

**Constructive Feedback**
- Feedback is a gift—receive it with openness
- Be specific and actionable in your feedback
- Focus on behavior, not personality
- Deliver feedback in a respectful, private manner
- Follow up to ensure understanding

**Difficult Conversations**
- Address issues early before they escalate
- Approach conversations with curiosity, not judgment
- Listen actively to understand the other perspective
- Focus on finding solutions together

### Resolving Conflicts

**Direct Communication**
- Address conflicts directly with the person involved
- Choose an appropriate time and private setting
- Listen to understand their perspective
- Work together to find a resolution

**Escalation Path**
- If direct resolution isn't working, involve your manager
- HR is available to mediate if needed
- We're committed to resolving conflicts fairly and respectfully
